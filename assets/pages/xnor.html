XNOR Gates

What is an XNOR Gate?

The XNOR Gate is a logic gate that performs the Exclusive NOR operation. Specifically, it outputs a logic high when there are an even number of high inputs and a logic low when there are an odd number of high inputs. Another way of looking at it is that different inputs produce a logic low, while equivalent inputs produce a logic high. Hence, it is also known as the material biconditional and is applied in circuits such as the implement similarity checker circuit. This logic gate can be denoted by the ⊙ symbol, e.g., Y = A ⊙ B.

Implementations of XNOR Gates

The XNOR Gate can be implemented in several ways, ranging from simple to complex. This section discusses some methods for implementing XNOR Gates in digital circuits.

XNOR Gate Symbol

The XNOR Gate, as its name suggests, is a combination of the XOR and NOT Gates. For that reason, its symbol is the XOR symbol with a complement bubble.

XNOR Gate Using Basic Logic Gates

The Boolean Expression of the XNOR Gate, Y = A ⊙ B, can be expanded to be an expression of the basic logic gates—the NOT, AND, as well as OR Gates. This results in the expression Y = AB + A’B’. Hence, it can be implemented with the basic logic gates corresponding to that expanded expression.

Trade-offs of an XNOR Gate

Advantages

  • XNOR Gates are known for their straightforward implementation.
  • XNOR Gates are purpose-built for equality testing.
  • XNOR Gates offer complementary output signals.
  • XNOR Gates simplify circuit designs that involve comparisons.
  • XNOR Gates play a crucial role in memory storage, improving data integrity and reliability.

Disadvantages

  • XNOR Gates are limited to equality comparisons and lack versatility for more complex logic operations.
  • Using XNOR Gates exclusively to construct complex circuits can be challenging and less efficient.
  • XNOR Gates require an even number of inputs, limiting their flexibility in various applications.
  • XNOR Gates are not well-suited for arithmetic operations.
