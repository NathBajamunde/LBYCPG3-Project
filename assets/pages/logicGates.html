Logic Gates

What are Logic Gates?

A Logic Gate is a digital circuit that allows or prevents signals from passing based on a specified condition. Specifically, these signals can represent two states, either true or false. Normally, the positive supply (+Vs) voltage represents the true state, while 0V represents the false state. However, this can be changed depending on the user's interpretation and the internal circuit configuration.

Categories of Logic Gates

Logic Gates are primarily identified by the function or operation they perform. For each type of logic gate, there are symbols formed by distinctive shapes that make them easy to identify and understand. The symbols utilized in this website are of the Traditional Type. Other notable symbols available include IEC (International Electrotechnical Commission) symbols. The primary purpose of Logic Gates is to bridge Boolean Algebra into reality through electrical circuits.

Basic Gates

Basic Logic Gates serve as the foundation of all higher-level logic gates. They are composed of the three operations in Boolean Algebra, which include negation (NOT), conjunction (AND), as well as disjunction (OR). These three serve as the base components and are combined to form the other types of gates.

Logic Gate Boolean Expression
NOT Gate X=A’
AND Gate X=A⋅B
OR Gate X=A+B

Universal Gates

Universal Gates are a special type of logic gate dubbed as such for their ability to form any other logic gate without needing the help of additional gate types. There are two available Universal Gates: the NAND Gate and the NOR Gate. As their names suggest, they are formed by combining the NOT Gate with the AND or OR Gate, respectively.

Logic Gate Boolean Expression
NAND Gate X=(A⋅B)’
NOR Gate X=(A+B)’

Exclusive Gates

Exclusive Gates are digital logic gates that produce an output based on the similarity or differences between the logic state of their inputs. There are two types of gates in this category: the XOR Gate and the XNOR Gate, both of which are also made from the Basic Gates.

Logic Gate Boolean Expression
XOR Gate X=A⊕B
XNOR Gate X=A⊙B

Truth Tables

Truth Tables are tables that illustrate the corresponding output states for every possible input combination. For that reason, they are excellent at showing the function of a logic gate. As mentioned earlier, the symbols used to represent logic states can vary. On this website, 1 and 0 will be used in the Truth Tables. The tables shown are examples of what a truth table can look like and what it can contain.
